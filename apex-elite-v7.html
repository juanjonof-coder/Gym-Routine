🧭 CARDIO DECISION ENGINE — Read This First Which · When · Why · Override Rules ▼
Three types of cardio in this program. Not interchangeable. Each has a specific physiological role assigned to specific days based on CNS stress, recovery runway, and training goal. Follow the table — it solves the sequencing problem for you.
Tier 1 · Foundation
LISS
Zone 2 · HR 120–140. Burns fat as primary fuel. Builds mitochondrial density. Recovers the body rather than stressing it. 30–40 min incline walk, bike, or rowing. Restorative — can run adjacent to any lifting session.
Tier 2 · Metabolic
HIIT
Zone 4–5 · All-out effort. Creates EPOC — elevated metabolism 12–24 hrs post-session. Max 20 min. Assault bike or sprints. Limited to 1 true session per week at this training volume — more is counterproductive.
Tier 3 · Athletic
ATHLETIC
Full-body functional conditioning. Saturday only — sleds, carries, jumps, sprints, circuits. This is what separates a gym physique from an athletic physique. Not replaceable by extra cardio.

Training days: add 150–200 kcal from carbs peri-workout. Rest day (Sunday): reduce carbs by 50–75g. Protein stays fixed at 285g every single day. Never drop fats below 68g — testosterone synthesis requires dietary fat.
Best Protein Sources
  • Chicken breast, turkey, lean bison — high protein per calorie
  • Salmon, tilapia, shrimp — protein + omega-3 combination
  • Whole eggs + egg whites — leucine-rich, highly bioavailable
  • Greek yogurt, cottage cheese — casein protein, gut health
  • Lean beef 93%+ — creatine, zinc, iron, B12 in one food
  • Whey isolate post-workout — fast leucine spike when needed
Meal Timing
  • Meal 1 (AM): 4 eggs + oats + fruit — protein + carbs to start
  • Pre-workout (60–90 min): 40g protein + 50–70g carbs whole food
  • Post-workout (within 60 min): Whey + banana or rice — fast leucine
  • Meal 3 (afternoon): Lean protein + complex carbs + vegetables
  • Meal 4 (evening): Lean protein + healthy fats + low-carb veg
  • Pre-bed optional: Casein or cottage cheese — overnight MPS
Hydration
  • Baseline: 3.5–4 litres daily minimum
  • Add 500–750ml per training hour to replace sweat
  • 500ml water immediately on waking — before coffee
  • Electrolytes on training days: sodium, potassium, magnesium
  • Coffee max 1–3 cups. None after 2PM — protect sleep quality
  • Alcohol max 1–2 drinks per week — disrupts testosterone and GH
Avoid These
  • Liquid calories — juice, soda, alcohol fill deficit with nothing useful
  • Ultra-processed carbs — spike and crash impair training quality
  • Late-night heavy carbs — disrupts cortisol and insulin rhythm overnight
  • Skipping meals — MPS requires consistent amino acid availability
  • Crash dieting below 2,400 kcal — destroys testosterone and muscle simultaneously
🛌 RECOVERY SYSTEM Sleep · Deload logic · Supplements · HRV ▼
Recovery is structured programming — not an afterthought. Connective tissue, tendons, and the endocrine system accumulate fatigue faster than muscles signal it. The training you do is only as good as the recovery that follows it.
🛌 Sleep
  • Target 7.5–9 hours. Below 7 hrs impairs testosterone and GH significantly.
  • Consistent bed and wake times — regulates cortisol rhythm
  • Room temp 65–68°F (18–20°C) — optimal for deep sleep and GH release
  • No screens 60 min before bed — blue light suppresses melatonin up to 50%
  • Magnesium glycinate 400mg + zinc 30mg before bed
💊 Core Supplements
  • Creatine monohydrate 5g daily — no loading needed. Every day, not just training days.
  • Omega-3 fish oil 3–4g daily — reduces systemic inflammation, supports joints
  • Vitamin D3 (3,000–5,000 IU) + K2 — testosterone support, immune function
  • Ashwagandha 600mg — reduces cortisol, supports testosterone at 40+
❤️ HRV Monitoring
  • Track HRV daily — WHOOP, Garmin, or Oura Ring
  • HRV 10–15% below 7-day average: reduce session intensity to 70%
  • HRV 20%+ below average: LISS or full rest only
  • Resting HR target: below 52 bpm = strong aerobic base
  • Trust HRV data over how you subjectively "feel"
🧊 Cold Exposure
  • Cold plunge or shower (50–59°F) 3–4× weekly
  • 2–5 minutes per session — benefits plateau after 6 min
  • Do NOT use immediately post-hypertrophy — wait 4–6 hours

📊 PROGRESS TRACKING 5 metrics · Weekly check-in · What actually matters ▼
Scale weight alone is the worst metric for recomp. Waist dropping while weight holds flat = fat loss with muscle retention. That is the goal. Use all five metrics together.

  • Weigh daily — log the 7-day average as your real trend
  • Target 0.5–1.0 lb/week fat loss in recomp

📅 16-WEEK PERIODIZATION 4 phases · Progressive overload · Long-term structure ▼
Four 4-week mesocycles. Volume and intensity vary deliberately across phases to drive continuous adaptation. Do not repeat Phase 1 indefinitely — the phases build on each other.
Phase 1 · Weeks 1–4 (ACTIVE)
  • Volume Base — 10–15 rep range, moderate loads, build capacity
  • Build metabolic stress and mitochondrial density
  • Establish movement quality and connective tissue conditioning
  • Week 4 = Deload
Phase 2 · Weeks 5–8
  • Intensification — shift to 6–10 rep range on compounds
  • Strength retention prioritized. Volume maintained via supersets.
  • Cardio volume holds steady — do not increase
  • Week 8 = Deload
Phase 3 · Weeks 9–12
  • Peak Output — 3–5 rep range on compounds, maximum tension
  • Most demanding phase — sleep and nutrition must be tight
  • Cardio volume peaks simultaneously with training intensity
  • Week 12 = Deload

  • Realization — W13 Deload, peak conditioning W14–15

  • Decision: new 16-week block or transition to maintenance
📆 12-WEEK CUT PHASING 3 phases · Calories · Cardio · Steps · Expected look ▼
The cut is phased so you get leaner without crashing performance or muscle mass. Each phase applies slightly more pressure — more cardio, lower calories, more steps — while protecting strength and fullness. Start Phase 1. Move to Phase 2 only when fat loss stalls or after 4 weeks.

Foundation

📈 PROGRESSION & ADJUSTMENT RULES How to progress lifts · When to adjust calories or cardio ▼
Double Progression — How to Progress Lifts on a Cut
On a cut, the win is maintaining performance while reps trend upward inside the target range. Never chase weekly weight jumps — chase rep quality and consistency first. Once all sets hit the top of the rep range cleanly, increase the weight modestly.
Week 1
Start at a challenging but manageable weight. Example: Incline DB Press 4 × 6 at chosen load. All reps clean, stop 1–2 reps before failure.
Week 2
Same weight. If energy and execution are good, push to 7 reps on the early sets. Do not chase it — let it happen naturally.
Week 3
Continue filling the 6–8 rep range with high-quality reps. The goal is every set hitting the top end cleanly before adding weight.
Week 4
All sets at 8 clean reps? Increase the weight modestly next session and restart the progression cycle. This is controlled, sustainable overload during a deficit.
Stalling
If reps drop or performance crashes for 2+ sessions, do not reduce weight immediately. First check: sleep, calories, stress load. If those are fine, reduce intensity techniques before touching load.
Intensity Techniques — Rules
When to Use Them
  • Compounds: stop 1–2 reps before failure — always. No exceptions.
  • Isolation exercises: one intensity technique on the final set only
  • Best options: drop set, rest-pause, slow eccentric, or stretch overload
  • Only use when recovery is solid — not on back-to-back hard days
  • If performance drops, remove intensity techniques before removing exercises
Deload Rule
  • Deload around Week 7 or 8 — reduce volume ~30%, keep intensity
  • Signs you need it early: persistent joint aches, flat pumps, declining motivation
  • Deload does not mean stopping — keep moving, reduce load and sets
  • After deload, performance typically bounces back above pre-deload levels
  • Never skip the deload to "stay on track" — it is the track
